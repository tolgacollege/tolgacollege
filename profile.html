Athlete Profile

Riverside High School, Class of 2028 · Nations Capital Swim Club (NCAP), Potomac Valley Swimming

Swimming background

  • Started competitive swimming in North Dakota with Dickinson Dolphin Swim Club and Dickinson High School.
  • Moved to Virginia and joined Nations Capital Swim Club (NCAP), one of the premier clubs in the country.
  • Top-ranked swimmer on the Riverside High School team for the second straight year.
  • Versatile scorer across IM, backstroke, breaststroke, and freestyle — can score in 5 or more individual SCY events.

Event identity

  • Primary identity: IM swimmer who's also a strong backstroker and breaststroker.
  • Backstroke is his strongest relay leg on both medley relays (100 back and 200 back).
  • 200 breaststroke ranked #61 nationally opens a second relay contribution on the breaststroke leg.
  • SwimIntel primary events: 200Y breaststroke, 200Y IM, 400Y IM.

Academic & career goals

  • 4.41 unweighted GPA at Riverside High School.
  • Goal: Doctor of Physical Therapy (DPT) with a sports medicine focus.
  • Undergraduate target majors: exercise science, kinesiology, or biology.
  • Also exploring Finance / Business as a secondary interest — not yet decided.

Personal details

  • Hometown: Leesburg, VA (moved from Dickinson, ND).
  • High school: Riverside High School, Class of 2028.
  • Club: Nations Capital Swim Club (NCAP Loudoun), LSC: Potomac Valley Swimming.
  • Open to programs in any region.
  • Family priority: schools with strong DPT and sports medicine clinical partnerships.
Family

Home support

Tolga is proud to call Lansdowne, VA home, surrounded by a close-knit family of four who are fully invested in his journey. His father brings 20+ years of management experience in oil and gas, now consulting for energy sector companies, while his mother works as a part-time Database Administrator. His older brother, a former high school and club swimmer himself, understands firsthand the dedication swimming demands. Together, the family is wholeheartedly committed to supporting Tolga's swimming career.
